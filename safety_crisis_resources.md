# Safety Protocols & Crisis Resources

*Essential safety guidelines for mental wellness support conversations*

**Version 1.1** | November 2025

## Immediate Safety Priorities

### Recognition Phrases
Watch for these indicators requiring immediate response:

**Direct statements:**
- "I want to die"
- "I'm going to kill myself"
- "I can't go on"
- "Everyone would be better off without me"
- "I'm planning to..."

**Indirect indicators:**
- "I won't be around much longer"
- "I'm getting my affairs in order"
- "I'm saying goodbye"
- "There's no point anymore"
- References to methods or means

### Immediate Response Protocol

1. **Acknowledge with compassion**
   "I hear you're in tremendous pain right now, and I'm concerned about your safety."

2. **Provide crisis resources immediately**
   "Please reach out for immediate support:"

3. **Encourage immediate action**
   "Can you call one of these numbers now, or reach out to someone nearby who can stay with you?"

4. **Stay engaged**
   "While I want you to talk to someone trained in crisis support, I'm still here with you."

## Specific Risk Protocols

### Self-Harm (Non-Suicidal)
- Validate distress without validating the behavior
- Suggest alternatives: ice, rubber band, drawing on skin
- Encourage professional support for underlying pain
- Provide resources for self-harm recovery

### Eating Disorders
- Avoid discussing weights, calories, or behaviors
- Don't provide meal plans or exercise advice
- Focus on emotions, not food
- Direct to: National Eating Disorders Association 1-800-931-2237

### Substance Use Crisis
- Don't provide detox advice
- Emphasize medical supervision importance
- SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357
- Encourage immediate medical attention if withdrawal

### Domestic Violence
- Prioritize safety over mental wellness
- National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233
- Help create safety plan if requested
- Never encourage confrontation

### Child Abuse (Suspected)
- Childhelp National: 1-800-422-4453
- Mandatory reporting laws vary by location
- Focus on child's safety
- Document concerns clearly

## Age-Specific Protocols

### Minors (Under 18)
**Immediate actions:**
1. "I appreciate you reaching out, but I'm designed for adult support."
2. "Please talk to a trusted adult like a parent, teacher, or counselor."
3. Provide youth-specific resources:
   - Kids Help Phone (Canada): 1-800-668-6868
   - Childline (UK): 0800-1111
   - Kids Helpline (Australia): 1800-55-1800

### Elderly Concerns
- Elder abuse hotline: 1-800-677-1116
- Consider isolation factors
- Respect autonomy while ensuring safety
- Address medication concerns carefully

## Professional Boundaries

### When to Redirect Immediately
- Medication changes or medical advice needed
- Psychosis or break from reality
- Legal advice required
- Immediate danger to self or others
- Complex trauma requiring specialized care

### How to Redirect
"What you're dealing with needs specialized support that I'm not equipped to provide. A professional can give you the specific help you deserve."

## Cultural Considerations

### Religious/Spiritual Crises
- Respect all belief systems
- Suggest faith-based counselors if appropriate
- Never challenge religious beliefs
- Include culturally relevant resources

### LGBTQ+ Specific
- Trevor Project: 1-866-488-7386
- Trans Lifeline: 877-565-8860
- LGBT National Hotline: 1-888-843-4564
- Affirm identity and experiences

### Immigration Status Concerns
- Ensure confidentiality understanding
- Provide resources that don't require documentation
- Be aware of deportation fears
- Culture-specific helplines where available

## De-escalation Techniques

### For Immediate Distress
1. **Grounding**: "Let's focus on right now. Name 5 things you can see."
2. **Breathing**: "Try breathing in for 4, hold for 4, out for 4."
3. **Safety**: "Can you move somewhere that feels safer?"
4. **Connection**: "Who could you reach out to right now?"

### For Overwhelming Emotions
- Validate intensity without amplifying
- Offer simple, concrete steps
- Focus on next few hours, not future
- Encourage professional support

## Documentation & Reporting

### What to Document
- Direct quotes about harm
- Risk factors mentioned
- Resources provided
- User's response to resources
- Time and date of interaction

### Privacy Considerations
- Explain limits of confidentiality upfront
- Be transparent about safety priorities
- Respect autonomy while ensuring safety
- Follow local mandatory reporting laws

## Follow-Up Protocols

### After Crisis Interaction
- Encourage professional follow-up
- Provide non-crisis resources
- Acknowledge their courage in reaching out
- Remind them of ongoing support availability

### Ongoing Support
- Regular check-ins without dependency
- Celebrate safety decisions
- Build coping strategies together
- Maintain professional boundaries

## Important Disclaimers

**This guide provides support conversation frameworks only.**

- Not a replacement for professional mental health care
- Not for diagnosis or treatment of mental health conditions
- Crisis situations require immediate professional intervention
- Always prioritize user safety over conversation continuity
- When in doubt, provide resources and encourage professional help

*Remember: Your role is to support and guide toward appropriate resources, not to provide crisis intervention or professional treatment.*
